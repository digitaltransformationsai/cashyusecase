Phase 1

Foundation and operating system

First step was not content. It was infrastructure. The AI employee was set up with a defined identity, rules of engagement, memory structure, escalation format, and clear knowledge of team roles, business priorities, and communication preferences.

Phase 3

Research operations and lead intelligence

A major workstream was building structured legal-market datasets by county and practice area. The process included broad collection, second-pass cleaning, public directory verification, bar cross-reference enrichment, and Google Sheet delivery.

Data collection

Built attorney lists by geography and practice area using public sources, then structured the data into spreadsheet-ready formats.

Enrichment

Cross-referenced public professional information, including bar details where available, and appended professional contact fields conservatively.

Delivery

Pushed cleaned outputs into a live Google Sheet with named tabs, frozen headers, filters, and downloadable CSV backups.

Practice-area and county sheet buildout

Delivered multiple sheet tabs and CSV exports spanning personal injury, estate planning, probate, family law, immigration, bankruptcy, and multi-county immigration lists. Where first-pass coverage was thin, broader second passes were run to improve realism and usefulness.

Phase 4

Meeting prep and strategic support

Beyond raw research, the role expanded into preparing concise briefings for business meetings — combining public profile research with operational pain-point analysis and suggested positioning.

Attorney / firm briefings

Prepared compact pre-meeting dossiers covering public profile, likely operational structure, administrative pain points, scheduling issues, and opportunities for automation or VA support.

Discovery support

Generated conversation talking points, discovery questions, and suggested positioning to keep meetings practical and focused on efficiency wins.

How it worked

Execution model

The engagement blended direct in-session execution for quick tasks with background runs for heavier research. Outputs were then packaged into attachments, sheets, or ready-to-use HTML deliverables.

01

Capture context

Define business goals, team roles, communication preferences, and project priorities before trying to optimize anything.

02

Execute fast

Ship first versions quickly: outlines, HTML drafts, research sheets, and briefings. Then refine with feedback instead of over-planning upfront.

03

Enrich and structure

Where surface-level data wasn’t enough, run a second pass: dedupe, enrich, verify public info, and organize into decision-ready formats.

04

Document the operating rules

Convert repeated preferences into workflow rules — including voice-note handling, memory, and reporting — so the system gets more useful over time.

Outcome

What this case study proves

An AI employee can do more than answer questions. With the right operating structure, it can support research, web production, spreadsheet enrichment, meeting preparation, workflow design, and day-to-day execution in a way that compounds over time.
